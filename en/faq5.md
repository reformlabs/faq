### Do you accept feature requests?

Yes. Reform Labs welcomes feature requests and suggestions from the community. Many improvements to software projects start as ideas shared by users or contributors, and we appreciate people who take the time to suggest ways to improve our tools. If you have a feature idea, you can submit it by opening a **GitHub Issue** or **GitHub Discussion** in the repository related to the project.

When submitting a feature request, it is helpful to include:

- A clear description of the feature you are proposing
- The problem or limitation the feature would solve
- A possible implementation approach (if you have one)
- Examples or use cases showing why the feature would be useful

Please understand that **submitting a feature request does not guarantee that it will be accepted or implemented**. Our team evaluates suggestions based on several factors, including:

- Alignment with the project's goals and philosophy
- Technical feasibility and complexity
- Long-term maintainability
- Compatibility with the existing architecture
- Overall benefit to the ecosystem and its users

In some cases, a feature request may be declined, postponed, or suggested to be implemented as an independent extension or external project.

We encourage open discussion around ideas, and community feedback often helps shape the direction of our projects.
